Star Clusters:

Star clusters are groups of stars that are held together by gravity. The stars in a cluster usually formed from the same cloud of gas and dust, so they are about the same age. There are two main types of star clusters: open clusters, which contain hundreds of stars, and globular clusters, which contain hundreds of thousands or even millions of stars.

Star clusters form when a large nebula collapses under the force of gravity. As the gas and dust come together, many stars are created at around the same time. These stars remain close to one another because their gravity keeps them connected, forming a cluster.

Star clusters are important because they help astronomers understand how stars are born, change over time, and eventually die. Since the stars in a cluster are the same age but have different masses, scientists can compare them to learn more about stellar evolution. They also help us learn about the history and structure of our galaxy.
